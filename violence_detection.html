Introduction

This project addresses the need for automated violence detection in forensic reports through the use of Optical Character Recognition (OCR). By converting handwritten reports into machine-readable text, we aim to facilitate analysis while preserving critical information about injuries and assault indicators.

Problem & Goal

Despite advancements in Electronic Health Records (EHR), forensic reports often remain handwritten. This project aims to leverage OCR technology to ensure that vital information, such as injury mechanisms and anatomical details, is accurately preserved and accessible for further analysis in violence cases.

Data & Workflow

We created synthetic reports for three case types: Domestic Violence, Physical Abuse, and Sexual Assault. OCR tools and Vision-based Large Language Models (Vision LLMs) were evaluated for their ability to extract and preserve information. Our workflow involved a comparative analysis of commercial OCR tools and open-source models like Llama-3.2-11B-Vision.

OCR Tools Evaluated

Tools like Google Cloud Vision and DocumentAI were assessed for accuracy and information retention in violence detection.

Metrics

We measured performance through Word Error Rates and Information Preservation Analysis, focusing on critical elements such as injury locations.

Key Results

The Cloud Vision API demonstrated the highest accuracy with a 0.17 Word Error Rate, and the Llama-3.2-11B-Vision model showed promise as a locally deployable alternative. These tools preserved 100% of anatomical details and achieved high accuracy in injury mechanism detection, crucial for violence identification.

Future Directions

We plan to improve performance by fine-tuning Vision LLMs like Llama-3.2-11B-Vision and exploring integration with EHR systems to facilitate automated violence detection. Developing specialized forensic tools will also be a priority.
